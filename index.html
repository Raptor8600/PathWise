How this is calculated
This uses the compound interest formula with monthly contributions. Each month, we calculate interest on the current balance, add it, and then add your monthly contribution.

How this is calculated
Inflation compoundly increases prices. "Future Cost" is what you'd need then to buy what $X buys today. "Today's Value" is what your $X today will effectively buy then.
Formula: FV = PV × (1 + r)^n
Example: At 3% inflation, $100 today buys what $103 will buy in one year.
Disclaimer: Inflation calculations are based on the historical concept of purchasing power. Actual future inflation rates are unpredictable. Not financial advice.
